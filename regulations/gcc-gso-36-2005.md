# Motor Vehicles - Methods of Test for Impact Strength - Part 1: Frontal Impact

**Regulated Area:** Frontal impact

**Applicability:** Applies to motor vehicles exported/registered in GCC member states when listed in GSO motor vehicle technical regulations; passenger relevance noted for passenger cars/M1.


## Key Compliance Intent

Maintain occupant survival space and manage crash energy in full, offset, or full-width frontal crashes.

## Primary Vehicle Systems and Components

Front rails, bumper beam, crush cans, dash/cowl, pedals, steering column, airbags, belts, seats, sensors, firewall.

## Failure Modes and Symptoms

Excessive HIC/chest/femur loads, excessive pedal or steering intrusion, airbag timing errors, dash intrusion, door jamming after crash.

## Related Regulations

FMVSS/CMVSS 208; UNECE R94/R137; GB 11551 and GB/T 20913; KMVSS Arts. 102/102-3; ADR 69/73; CONTRAN 910; GSO 36/40; Japan Article 18.

## Engineering Considerations

Listed in GSO MY2027 technical regulations; passenger crash structure/restraint relevance.
