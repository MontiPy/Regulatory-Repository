## Article 50 — Indirect Vision Devices

① Motor vehicles shall be equipped with indirect vision devices of one of the following types to enable the driver to observe the traffic situation: [Amended 2017-01-09, 2018-07-11]

1. Mirror-based indirect vision devices shall be installed in accordance with Attached Table 5-6 and shall meet the field of view requirements in Attached Table 5-7. However, for ultra-compact vehicles, the installation and field of view of indirect vision devices shall meet the standards in Attached Table 50.

2. Camera monitor system-based indirect vision devices shall be installed in accordance with Attached Tables 5-6 and 5-8 and shall meet the field of view requirements in Attached Table 5-7.

② School vans for children (excluding those where the engine is located in front of the driver's seat) shall additionally be equipped with an indirect vision device capable of detecting obstacles directly in front of the vehicle body. [Amended 2017-01-09]

③ Indirect vision devices installed on the left and right sides of school vans for children shall be structured so that an observation rod of 30 mm diameter and 1,200 mm height, placed 300 mm perpendicularly from the vehicle body at the point of the last-to-close part of the door (or the symmetrical point on the side without a door), is fully visible when viewed from 635 mm above the driver's seating reference point. [Added 2008-01-14, 2011-08-31, 2014-02-21, 2017-01-09]

④ Where a wide-angle external rear-view mirror or imaging device with an average radius of curvature of 200 mm or more and a reflective surface of 10,000 mm² or more is additionally installed to the indirect vision devices under paragraph ① and meets the standards in paragraph ③, this is deemed to satisfy the requirements for school vans for children. [Added 2011-08-31, 2017-01-09]
